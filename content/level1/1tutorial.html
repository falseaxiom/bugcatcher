Hello again! Thimis Hintz here.

Welcome to the first true environment! Before embarking on this journey, there are some special critters here that you should learn about.

The first are brackets.

Typically, brackets will enclose methods, method arguments, array contents, et cetera.

These little guys always travel in pairs--one open, one closed--and they must always be of matching shapes.

The second are semicolons.

They usually like to hang out at the end of most lines, but are also commonly inside for-loops, separating conditions.

The third are typos.

These can happen pretty much anywhere--a misspelled method, a missing return type, a variable declared as "temp" but called as "timp"... to name a few!

There are 4 environments in this level; the first 3 will be buggy versions of the same code, to make spotting bugs a little easier.

Well, off you go! Let's see how fast you can catch these bugs!
