Today, we'll be taking a look at variable scope, and the bugs that can arise from it.

A variable can be declared almost anywhere--inside or outside of methods, in for-loops, in if-statements, etc.

However, its scope is confined by the nearest curly {} brackets.

For example, if you declare a variable inside an if-statement, you can't use it outside of the if-statement!

Think of the brackets as a little fence that contains the variable. You can't call the variable outside the fence, because it has no way of getting to you.

It may seem simple, but even the best bugcatchers fall prey to errors involving variable scope.
